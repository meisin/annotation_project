Event Modality and Polarity

Event extraction (event triggers and event arguments) is simply insufficient to represent the events correctly. In order to have an accurate interpretation of events, it is important to distinguish actual events from speculated/forecasted/anticipated events under the Modality attribute. It is also important to separate positive events from negated ones under the Polarity attribute.

  1. Modality (ASSERTED or OTHER) -
    An event is annotated as ASSERTED when the author or speaker makes reference to it as though it was a real occurrence. All other Events, including believed events, hypothetical events, commanded / requested event, speculated / forecasted event are annotated as OTHER.
    1. Modality ASSERTED: Washington issued a statement confirming US's sanctions on Iran.
    2. Modality OTHER: The market expects US to sanction Iran.

  2. Polarity (POSITIVE or NEGATIVE) -
    An event is NEGATIVE when it is explicitly indicated that the Event did not occur. The non-occurrence of the Event must be explicitly and intentionally communicated. All other Events are POSITIVE.
    1. Polarity POSITIVE: OPEC countries cut oil supplies.
    2. Polarity NEGATIVE: OPEC countries refused cut oil supplies.
